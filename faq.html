Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers about growing restaurant sales through DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, Toast, and direct online ordering.

Services & Fit

What we manage, who we help, and how we fit into your business.

What exactly does Blender Digital do?

We manage and grow your restaurant’s online ordering channels. That includes DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, Toast, and other direct-ordering platforms.

Our work can include promotional campaigns, Sponsored Listings, menu and storefront optimization, review strategy, reporting, and ongoing performance improvements. We take ownership of the work restaurant owners rarely have time to manage consistently.

Is Blender Digital a traditional restaurant marketing agency?

Not exactly. Most agencies focus on social media, websites, photography, or broad advertising. Those services can be valuable, but they often stop before the customer actually places an order.

We focus on the platforms where customers are already deciding what to eat and where to order. Our goal is to improve visibility, conversion, order volume, average order value, customer retention, and the financial performance of your online ordering channels.

Which restaurant delivery platforms do you manage?

We primarily work with DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub, Toast, direct online ordering systems, and catering platforms such as ezCater when applicable.

The strategy depends on where your restaurant currently sells, how each storefront performs, and where the strongest growth opportunities exist.

Do you work with independent restaurants, franchises, and restaurant groups?

Yes. We work with single-location restaurants, individual franchisees, multi-unit operators, and restaurant groups.

A single location may need a stronger foundation. A multi-location group may need centralized reporting, consistent campaign governance, and location-specific adjustments. We do not assume every store should run the same campaign simply because the locations share a brand.

Who is Blender Digital a good fit for?

We are a strong fit for operators who already sell through online ordering platforms, want more consistent management, care about both sales and profitability, and are willing to test and improve their offers.

We may not be the right fit for a restaurant expecting guaranteed overnight growth, refusing to make any changes, or hoping discounts alone will solve deeper operational problems.

Results & Timelines

What happens after onboarding and how performance is measured.

Can you really increase our DoorDash and Uber Eats sales?

That is the goal, but we do not promise overnight results or guarantee a specific revenue number.

We improve the parts of your storefront and marketing strategy that influence sales: menu structure, product visibility, promotions, Sponsored Listings, customer targeting, reviews, pricing, offer design, and ongoing campaign adjustments.

The difference is that we do not launch a few promotions and disappear. We continue reviewing performance, identifying problems, and adjusting the strategy.

How quickly can we expect to see results?

Many restaurants begin seeing measurable changes within the first 30 to 60 days, particularly when there are clear opportunities involving promotions, menu organization, Sponsored Listings, or storefront quality.

More substantial growth usually requires consistent optimization over several months. Timing is affected by current sales, local competition, ratings, pricing, food quality, operating hours, delivery availability, platform visibility, and promotional budget.

What happens during the first month?

We review sales and order trends, average order value, menu structure, photos and descriptions, current promotions, Sponsored Listings, customer ratings, platform fees, availability, competitor positioning, and new-versus-returning customer behavior.

Then we identify quick wins, correct avoidable issues, launch or improve campaigns, and establish a baseline for measuring future performance.

Do you guarantee results?

No responsible agency should guarantee a specific sales result. We control the strategy, analysis, setup, optimization, and quality of our work. We do not control customer demand, food quality, preparation times, order accuracy, platform algorithms, competition, weather, or store closures.

What we do promise is active management, clear recommendations, ongoing testing, and decisions built around actual performance rather than guesswork.

How do you measure success?

We look beyond one headline sales number. Depending on the goal, we may track gross sales, net payout, order volume, average order value, new and returning customers, promotion redemptions, ad spend, return on ad spend, ratings, cancellations, availability, menu performance, and platform fees.

A customer-acquisition campaign should not be judged exactly like a campaign designed to improve repeat orders or profitability.

Promotions, Ads & Profitability

How we pursue growth without ignoring margins.

What kinds of restaurant promotions do you create?

Offers may include BOGO campaigns, spend-and-save discounts, new-customer offers, lapsed-customer win-back campaigns, free delivery, happy-hour offers, item-specific discounts, day-of-week promotions, seasonal campaigns, and catering offers.

We do not believe every restaurant should run the same discount. The offer needs to fit your menu, margins, customer behavior, and business objective.

Will promotions hurt our profit margins?

They can when they are poorly structured. Aggressive discounts become dangerous when no one accounts for food cost, platform fees, minimum order requirements, customer eligibility, or promotion stacking.

We evaluate more than gross sales. We also consider payout, average order value, customer type, redemption behavior, and likely long-term value. The goal is not impressive-looking revenue that quietly loses money on every order.

Do you manage Sponsored Listings and in-app advertising?

Yes. We review budget, targeting, return on ad spend, order volume, campaign structure, and whether paid visibility is creating meaningful incremental value.

Sponsored Listings should not run on autopilot. Advertising performs best when the storefront, menu, photos, ratings, and offer are ready to convert the extra traffic.

Do you optimize the online menu too?

Yes. Your online menu is not merely a copy of the printed menu; it is one of your restaurant’s most important sales pages.

We may recommend changes to categories, item order, names, descriptions, pricing, modifiers, add-ons, bundles, best-seller placement, photos, upsells, high-margin items, and catering packages.

Do you help with restaurant reviews and ratings?

Yes. Ratings influence customer trust, conversion, and often platform visibility. We help create a consistent review-management process, identify recurring complaints, draft appropriate responses, and use review trends to uncover menu or operational problems.

We cannot remove legitimate negative reviews or guarantee a particular rating.

Working Together

Access, workload, communication, pricing, and next steps.

Will we need to hire another employee?

No. Blender Digital acts as an extension of your team. We handle strategy, platform management, campaign planning, performance reviews, and ongoing recommendations.

Your team may occasionally need to approve offers, confirm pricing, provide menu information or photos, and address operational issues—but you should not need a full-time employee just to manage the delivery platforms.

How much time will this require from me?

Usually not much after onboarding. We need access to the relevant platforms, information about your menu and margins, and a clear understanding of your goals.

After that, communication focuses on approvals, major recommendations, operational issues, and performance updates. We are here to take work off your plate, not create another job for you.

Do you need access to our DoorDash, Uber Eats, or Toast accounts?

Yes. To properly manage and optimize your accounts, we need approved access to the relevant merchant dashboards. We recommend creating appropriate user access whenever the platform allows it rather than casually sharing personal login credentials.

Can you work with our existing marketing agency or social media team?

Yes. We often complement internal teams, social media managers, photographers, web agencies, and paid media partners. They may focus on awareness and content; we focus on turning online ordering platforms into stronger sales channels.
